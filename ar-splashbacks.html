AR SPLASHBACKS

WHY?

I worked for a splashback company at the time and they were thinking about ways to grab more customers in the form of AR as Dulux (famous paint brand) had an AR app where you could use your mobile camera, point it at a wall, choose a colour and it would make that wall look like it was painted in the colour you selected. Looking about I came across an extension for Aframe (3D Javascript library) called AR.js and I made a prototype where the customer print out a marker (marker based AR), stick it to their wall (where they wanted their splashback), point their phone at it and it would make it look like they had a splashback installed via AR, the fact the customer had the hassle of printing out a marker was the reason it never became more than a prototype(the limitations of web based AR at the moment)

HOW?

  • Customer prints out provided AR marker.
  • Customer attaches marker to position of where splashback would go.
  • Now they enter the splashback measurements (height/width).
  • Next AR.js fires up, customer is prompted to allow access to mobile camera, AR.js takes the passed measurement variables and applies height/width to the 3D representation of a splashback (its just a square 3D plain).
  • AR.js detects the marker from camera stream and attaches the 3D object to it in 3D space, giving the illusion of an actual splashback on their wall (exactly what augmented reality is).
  • To choose colour there is a scroll-able list of colours at the bottom that when clicked on, passes the div colour to AR.js object and applies that colour as a texture, so the customer can decide on a colour within an AR representation.
  • The 3D plain (augmented splashback), also has a cube-map reflection attached to it, giving the "splashback" a glass look to it and making it more immersive for the customer.
